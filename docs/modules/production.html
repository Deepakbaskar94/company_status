Production Planning Challenges

What happens without connected BOMs and work order management:

❌ BOM management is manual

Bill of Materials are scattered in Excel, making it hard to calculate material requirements accurately.

❌ Material shortages stop production

No automatic explosion of BOMs means you don't know what materials are needed until too late.

❌ Work orders lack visibility

WO creation and progress tracking are manual, so production status is always unclear.

❌ Material reservations conflict

Multiple work orders claim the same materials with no centralized system to prevent double-allocation.

❌ Scrap and yield tracking fails

Without WO completion tracking, you can't measure actual yield or identify production inefficiencies.

❌ Sales orders stall

No connection between SO, BOM, WO, and QC means order fulfillment is unpredictable.

Key Workflows

How production flows in Company Status:

SO to WO

Sales order confirmed → BOM exploded → Material requirement calculated → Work orders auto-created

WO Release

WO released → Materials reserved (FEFO) → Batches updated to "reserved" → Material issued to floor

WO Completion

Production complete → Finished goods batch created → Final QC triggered → SO ready for delivery
